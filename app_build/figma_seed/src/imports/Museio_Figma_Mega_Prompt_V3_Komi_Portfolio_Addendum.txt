==================================================
DESIGN PRINCIPLES FOR THE PORTFOLIO SYSTEM
==================================================

1. THUMB-FIRST EDITING
The creator should be able to edit the portfolio on a phone with one hand.
Design with lower-screen action zones, bottom sheets, sticky action docks, and large tap targets.
Important actions should live in the bottom 40 to 55 percent of the screen whenever possible.

2. LIVE PREVIEW ALWAYS
Editing must feel direct and visual. The creator should always feel close to the final result.
Support Edit, Preview, and Live modes with fast switching.

3. THEMES, NOT JUST FORMS
Portfolio building should feel like selecting a vibe, not filling out a boring CMS.
Let creators shape mood, color, media treatment, card style, edge treatment, typography, and CTA tone.

4. STORYTELLING OVER STACKING
Do not render the portfolio as a dead vertical list of generic blocks.
Create rhythm, contrast, pacing, reveal moments, and hero moments.
Treat sections like scenes in a story.

5. BOOKING SHOULD BE NATIVE
Public viewers should be able to admire the artist and immediately book or inquire without the experience feeling like they left the portfolio.
Booking is part of the story, not a detached form.

6. MOTION WITH PURPOSE
Motion should communicate hierarchy, focus, cause and effect, polish, and personality.
No gimmicky over-animation. Avoid chaos.
Think premium product motion, not novelty motion.

7. HIGH TRUST CONVERSION
For clients and venues, the public page must feel beautiful but also trustworthy, credible, and easy to act on.
Use testimonials, brand logos, key facts, response expectations, availability snippets, verified states, and clear CTA structure.

==================================================

MOTION PRINCIPLES
- refined
- smooth
- layered
- light but memorable
- confident
- not childish
- not noisy

MANDATORY MOTION PATTERNS
1. Hero reveal on page load
2. Section fade / slide / rise entrances
3. Sticky CTA dock appearing after scroll threshold
4. Theme switch morph
5. Block add animation in builder
6. Reorder animation with spring feel
7. Publish success burst / glow confirmation
8. Media card hover / press lift behavior
9. Bottom sheet rise with background blur
10. Gallery image opening into lightbox
11. Collapsing header on public scroll
12. Analytics cards count-up or staged reveal feel

RECOMMENDED MOTION TOKENS
- fast: 120 to 160 ms
- standard: 200 to 260 ms
- expressive: 320 to 420 ms
- use ease out, spring-like feel, and soft overshoot where appropriate

DO NOT OVERDO
Avoid:
- spinning gimmicks
- noisy particle effects
- constant pulsing everywhere
- motion that slows booking
- unreadable moving type
